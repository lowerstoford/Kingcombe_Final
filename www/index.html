Through the garden to Pound Cottage

The Long Walk takes us through some of the most attractive and interesting areas of the Kingcombe Meadows Nature Reserve. On leaving the Centre walk through the vegetable garden where many of the vegetables used in the cafe are grown. Here you come to Pound Cottage which was restored by Dorset Wildlife Trust in 1989 and is one of the original small cottages of Lower Kingcombe. This was occupied until *****, by *****.

Cross the road opposite Pound Cottage and through the field gate on to the reserve .

Onto the nature reserve

This field you are entering is called ‘Thorn Close’. All the fields on the reserve are named and many of these names have ancient origins. They can be seen on the 1844 Tithe map of Kingcombe Farm. This map shows a remarkably similar pattern of small fields that we have today. We will see as we follow this route how the reserve preserves a unique and beautiful landscape of former times that has been lost from most of our countryside.  Picture of tithe map.
In April and May we see a fine display of Lady’s Smock & orange tip butterflies

Follow the path across the field into the next narrow field ‘Cleaves’.  Primroses and dog violets abound.
